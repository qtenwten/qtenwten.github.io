Compound Interest Calculator

Estimate savings, investment growth, and recurring contributions online

Helpful Guide

A simple way to model long-term growth

Use this compound interest calculator to estimate how savings, investments, or deposits can grow over time. Enter a starting amount, annual rate, time period, and optional monthly contribution to see both future value and total interest earned.

What the calculator shows:

  • Future value based on different compounding frequencies
  • Monthly contribution support for ongoing savings plans
  • A simple chart showing capital growth over time
  • A breakdown of deposits versus earned interest
  • A fast way to compare saving and investing scenarios

Compound interest formula:

A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t)

  • A — final amount
  • P — initial amount
  • r — annual interest rate (in decimal form)
  • n — number of interest postings per year
  • t — number of years

Where this tool is useful:

Long-term saving: Check how a lump sum can grow over years with annual, monthly, or daily compounding.

Monthly investing: Add a recurring contribution to see how steady deposits change the final balance over time.

Scenario planning: Compare interest rates, time periods, and compounding frequency before choosing a savings or investment option.

FAQ

How do I calculate compound interest online?

Enter your starting amount, annual rate, time period, and compounding frequency. The calculator will show the projected future value and interest earned.

Can I include monthly contributions?

Yes. Add a monthly contribution to estimate how recurring deposits affect long-term growth.

Is this useful for savings and investments?

Yes. It works well for savings goals, investment forecasts, deposits, and other long-term planning scenarios.

What is the difference between simple and compound interest?

Compound interest grows on both the original amount and previously earned interest, while simple interest grows only on the original amount.
